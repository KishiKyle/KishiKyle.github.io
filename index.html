Explanation

This is a supplement of Galperin billiard model which show cases an interesting method of calculating PI, check out the full theory here:

The model:

Suppose there are 2 objects m1 and m2.

m2 is moving frictionlessly towards m1 at some arbitary velocity.

Behind m2 sits a perfectly elastic wall.

The behavior:
m1 collides with m2 in a perfectly elastic manner.

m2 is pushed away and collides with the wall.

m2 will thus continue to collide with the wall and m1 until the velocity of m1 shifts to the opposite direction.

The interesting part:

If the mass of m1 is 100n times the mass of m2:

Then the number of collisions that m2 has with the wall and m1 will equate to PI up to a decimal accuray of n.

PI = (collisions/10n)
